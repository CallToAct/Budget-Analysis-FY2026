Complete Source Documentation

The Standard: What Should Have Happened

Congressional Research Service. "Submission of the President's Budget in Transition Years." RS20752, updated 28 July 2021.
Establishes norms for first-year budgets and delays under incoming presidents since 1990.
Office of Management and Budget. "Historical Tables, Budget of the U.S. Government." Executive Office of the President.
Used to show consistency and punctuality in non-transition years.
Office of Management and Budget. "President's Budget FY2026 Request." The White House.
Official topline budget proposal—used to compare against justification delays.

The Exception: Departments That Actually Submitted

Office of Management and Budget. "The President's Fiscal Year 2026 Skinny Budget." The White House, 2 May 2025.
U.S. Department of Education. "Congressional Budget Justification Fiscal Year (FY) 2026." ED.gov, 4 June 2025.
One of the few full justifications publicly released.
U.S. Department of Homeland Security. "Congressional Budget Justification Fiscal Year (FY) 2026." DHS.gov, 12 June 2025.
One of the few full justifications publicly released.
U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs. "FY2026 Budget Highlights." VA.gov, May 2025.
Early partial justification; useful contrast against "pending" departments.

The Problem: Cabinet Secretaries Admitting Missing Documentation

Complete citations for all congressional hearings and testimony available upon request. Sources include Senate and House Appropriations Committee hearings from May-June 2025 where cabinet secretaries acknowledged incomplete budget justifications.

The Disconnect: Congress Moving Forward Anyway

House Committee on Appropriations. "FY2026 Defense Appropriations Bill Approved by Committee." Appropriations.House.gov, 12 June 2025.
Signals House appropriations underway even before Cabinet justifications are fully submitted.
U.S. House of Representatives. H. Con. Res. 14—Establishing the Congressional Budget for Fiscal Year 2025 and Setting Levels for Fiscal Years 2026 Through 2034. 119th Congress, 1st session, passed 25 Feb. 2025.
House-adopted budget resolution framing top-line spending targets.
U.S. Senate. S. Con. Res. 7—A Concurrent Resolution Setting Forth the Congressional Budget for Fiscal Year 2025 Through 2034. 119th Congress, 1st session, agreed to in Senate 21 Feb. 2025.
Senate counterpart establishing FY2026 enforcement limits.
